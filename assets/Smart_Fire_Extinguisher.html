Smart Fire Extinguisher

Autonomous sprinkler system that detects, pinpoints, and suppresses fires using real-time computer vision and robotics.

  • Role: Systems Engineer & ML Developer
  • Timeline: 2024
  • Tech: Raspberry Pi, YOLOv5, NodeMCU32-S, Arduino, Python
  • Repository: github.com/NizarSH98/SmartFireExtinguisher

Challenge

Traditional sprinkler systems flood entire spaces, wasting water and damaging equipment while still missing critical hotspots. The goal was to build a targeted fire-response system that could monitor a room, detect ignition sources, and extinguish fires with precision in seconds.

Solution Architecture

The solution combines computer vision, robotics, and IoT coordination to detect and extinguish fires. A YOLOv5 model running on a Raspberry Pi continuously scans the room via an RGB camera and thermal sensor mounted on a servo rig. When fire is detected, coordinates are shared with a NodeMCU32-S that orients a dual-servo robotic arm, positioning a high-pressure sprinkler aimed at the flame. An Arduino relay triggers the pump and monitors extinguishing progress.

  • Raspberry Pi camera & FLIR thermal module: Sweeps the room in 5° increments to capture RGB and heat signatures.
  • YOLOv5 inference: Identifies flame boundaries and sends coordinates to the control bus.
  • Robotic arm targeting: MG995 servos align the nozzle for pinpoint suppression.
  • High-pressure sprinkler: Pulses water only where heat remains, minimizing collateral damage.

Impact

  • Reduced water usage by activating only in affected zones.
  • Delivered sub-second detection-to-response times in lab simulations.
  • Protected sensitive electronics by limiting the spray radius to confirmed heat signatures.
